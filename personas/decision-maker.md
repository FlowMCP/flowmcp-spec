## Biography

Anders grew up in Stockholm, studied CS at KTH, and in 2002 co-founded his first company with two classmates — an online university-course rating service, sold to a Swedish media group in 2008. After that, six years at Spotify on the search-tech team (joined as engineer, left as engineering manager), then four years as CTO of an older Scandinavian SaaS company, where he migrated the stack from legacy to cloud-native. Since 2023 he's been CTO of his current company — marketing automation, four AI engineers on the team, just closed Series C. He's evaluating FlowMCP for the 2026 AI roadmap personally, because he won't push it onto his team without understanding it himself. He's conservative on stack decisions — not out of fear, but out of responsibility for 180 salaries and 30 million USD of investor capital.

## Primary goals

1. Understand "what is X?" in 60 seconds — one clear, jargon-light definition above the fold
2. Find trust signals: MIT license, GitHub stars (at least three digits), production logos, conference talks
3. See the roadmap and the team — are the next 12 months visible, is there a face behind the project?
4. Assess risks — abandonment, lock-in, license issues, maintainer bus factor
5. Compare with in-house build / competition — what does in-house maintenance cost vs adoption?

---

## Pain points

1. Vague promises and buzzwords ("AI-native", "revolutionary", "next-generation" without substance)
2. Missing or unclear roadmap — he needs to show his team at least 12 months of plan
3. No visible team on the website — who's behind the project, what's the bus factor?
4. No production proof (no customer logos, no case studies, only hackathon demos)
5. License ambiguity — if the LICENSE file is missing or is GPL, his legal team is out

---

## Likes

1. "What is X?" section above the fold with a jargon-light definition (max 2 sentences)

## When would he become a fan?

If the landing clearly says what FlowMCP is in 60 seconds, the MIT license is visible, a roadmap with quarters exists, the team is shown with faces, and at least three production logos are visible. Then he gives the green light for a pilot phase and puts FlowMCP on the AI roadmap.

## When would he walk away?

If the landing leads with buzzwords, if the roadmap is missing, if no team is visible, or if the only trust signals are hackathon awards. Then the decision is negative in 90 seconds and he doesn't re-open it.
